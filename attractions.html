Attractions

Norfolk Island is filled with attractions. If you love the outdoors and beaches, then you must go swimming at Emily Bay. Take this one step further and enjoy a glass bottom boat tour of the Bay guided by the Christian family, descendants of the Pitcairn Island mutineers that rebelled against Captain Bligh and the British Crown.


The island is replete with fascinating stories and dark historic tales. Learn about the islands penal colony past by exploring the superbly preserved Kingston quarters which are listed as a world heritage site. You'll also discover more about Norfolk's fascinatingly unique past at the local museum houses a treasure trove of intriguing artifacts. Or about one cunning convict whom evaded his capturers by hiding in a hollowed out tree.

The island's alluring scenic wonders redeem the island from it's turbulent colonial past. While the total are of the island is a meagre 35km squared, national parks and reserves exist in abundance. The most iconic of these must see wonders include the 100 Acres Reserve, the Anson Bay Reserve, the Ball Bay Reserve and the Bumboras Reserve. Or learn an invaluable lesson about the perils of how humans can drastically devastate nature by taking a short boat trip over to the terracotta red and barren Phillip Island.


A dinner at at one of locals is another great way to experience genuine Norfolk living. on their island haven. Or, you could add to your itinerary a guided nature tour of the island's stunning bird sanctuaries and coastal marine life.
