Things To Do

There are no shortage of diversions to pass the time away on Norfolk Island. For the active traveller, the sports and outdoor options are limitless. If you are a golf aficionado, then it will be hard to find a golf course which rivals that on picturesque Norfolk Island. Players will be treated to spectacular views of the historic penal camp at Kingston while also enjoying priceless views of the rumbling Pacific Ocean.


Norfolk Island is a recreations paradise. There are plenty of great spots go fishing, swimming or surfing on the island along with a host of other aquatic activities. If you prefer the land, hire a bike and explore the countless charming sites on offer. Or, join the locals in a game of squash, tennis or lawn bowls. You'll be made to feel very welcome. If you're feeling inspired, clime to the summit of Mt Pitt and take in a bird's eye view of the amazing panormaic views or priceless scenic vistas you'll talking about for the rest of your life. And don't forget to take part in an island feast. Norfolk Islanders love their BBQs and their fish frys.

If you are deeply into culture, then you may notice the Islanders saying some funny things. Don't be offended because you might just be hearing, Norfuk. This language can only be found on this island and it is a variant of Pitkern spoken on far off Pitcairn Island. These languages are a blend of eighteenth century English and Tahitian which was formed from the mutineers of the Bounty whom rebelled against the British and returned back to marry local women in Tahiti. They later fled to Pitcairn and later were invited to settle on Norfolk Island.


Norfolk Island plays hosts to regular markets which sell exquisite crafts and other locally produced goods. This is where you'll really get a taste of authentic Norfolk Island culture and cuisine. The main village in Burnt Pine has a bounty of wonderful shops where one can get a variety of glorious goods at great duty free prices. Treat yourself to a beauty pamper pack or stroll through one of Norfolk's iconic art galleries. The Islanders are very passionate about their identity and culture.
